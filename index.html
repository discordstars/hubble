  • After discovering that other galaxies exist, he also created a way to classify galaxies, called the Galaxy morphological classification.
  • This system was of course later expanded upon, but layed the foundations for the modern galaxy classification and has helped modern physicists understand the Universe.
all_out Other Galaxies
  • Edwin Hubble's work proved to astronomers that there are other galaxies outside of the milky way. At the time of the completion of the Hooker Telescope, the widely-accepted view of the Universe was that it consisted only of the Milky Way Galaxy.
  • Using this telescope, he used the methods he knew to find and use Cepheid variables — stars that pulsate at a frequent interval and can be used to determine the distance of surrounding objects. Using these stars, he proved that some nebulae observed by astronomers were simply too far away to be part of our own galaxy.
  • His discovery is seen as one of the most important discoveries in Astrophysics, and has lead to a much clearer understanding of the universe and changed the way people at the time and people now see the Universe.
